Formula 1 Qualifying Dashboards

Formula 1 is a challenging sport to quantify in terms of driver performance because a driver can only go as fast as the car allows, while the car can only reach its full potential if the driver pushes it to the limit. Another unique aspect of F1 is that a driverâ€™s teammate is often their biggest rival, as they are the only other driver with the same machinery. This is why comparing performance between teammates is one of the best indicators of driver skill in the sport.

For this analysis, I focused on qualifying sessions, as they provide the clearest view of which drivers can extract the most from their cars. Qualifying is less affected by traffic, and nearly all drivers use the same fresh tire compounds. The dashboards below include interactive filters to explore qualifying performances, starting with the 2023 season up to Belgium 2025.

Please note that I used each driver's fastest lap time from the session. While a driver may set a faster time, their official qualifying position can be lower due to the multi-stage qualifying format. Finally, track order in the dashboards follows the 2025 F1 calendar, so some timelines may appear slightly out of order since the race schedule changes from year to year.
